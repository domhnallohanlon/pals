Carina Davidson

Carina has worked with PALS since 2012. She is also admitted as a solicitor in England and Wales. Carina graduated from UCD with a BA (Hons) in Economics and English. She was awarded Distinction in a Diploma in Mediation for her dissertation in the area of Family Law. She has completed Certificates in Banking Law and Practice, Aviation Leasing and Financing and White Collar Crime. She has also received a commendation from the Law Society for her Professional Practice Financial Management examination. Carina works with Ronald Lynam in the criminal department and practices criminal defence litigation at District Court, Circuit Court, High Court, Central Criminal Court and Court of Criminal Appeal levels and Road Traffic Act offences. Her other area of practice is family law at District Court level (Access, Guardianship, Maintenance, Domestic Violence) at all District Courts within the Dublin Metropolitan District, Wicklow and Wexford areas. Carina also volunteers in the Employment Law Clinic at Free Legal Advice evenings in Dun Laoghaire Citizens Information Centre.

Elder Law

Justin McKenna heads up our division of Elder Law. The basic rights of senior members of our community are under threat every day from the most surprising source, their own family. This can manifest itself as financial abuse in the simplest of ways. For instance, a persuasive son or daughter may suggest that Credit Union savings be nominated in his or her favour. This is called “exercising undue influence”. It can be tackled with foresight and action. The most effective tool in the drawer is the enduring power of attorney. You will find this under “The law explained” on our home page. A will is another instrument giving power to the elderly. In each case the secret of the document is the appointment; and, putting the right person in charge in the event of certain circumstances arising. Give Justin a call in total confidence.
